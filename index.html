My name is Noah Bradley and I am a writer, game designer, and artist. I spent the last four years developing the skills necessary to work as a game developer. Most of this time has been spent as a student, exploring my interests and figuring out what excites me most about game development. During my time, I have fallen in love with story telling. I find that every mechanic, art asset, line of dialogue, level design choice, encounter, and everything that goes into the game works to tell a story and give the player an experience. Because I believe in this, I have honed my writing skills, my 3d art skills, and my game design skills. With these skills I make games and tell stories.

I have an itch page for the games I made as a student at the Academy of Interactive Entertainment, where I earned my certificate in game design. Since then I have continued to work on my personal projects. Of these, I am very proud of my Table Top Role Playing Game (TTRPG), Tales From Wyrd. I made this game together with my friends. As the Dungeon Master for our group, I was tired of running Dungeons and Dragons games. I wanted a more narrative experience, where my prep time was spent building out the story and not coming up with stat blocks for monsters. I also wanted my players to focus more on roleplaying and backstory. Tales From Wyrd aims to do both, with a simplistic combat and systems that reward roleplaying. I made this game over the course of a year and released an edited version on itch.io. While I still make edits from time, I have moved on to developing a new game in unreal engine 5. The game is called Star Free, a bullet hell platformer inspired by Ikaruga and Armored Core 6. In it you pilot the mech Albatross, flying through battlefields and destroying giant robots. The game is a work in progress and I look forward to sharing updates.
